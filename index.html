Iraxes

What is Iraxes?

Iraxes is, in essence, a political quiz that attempts to assign percentages for 7 different political axes. You will be presented by a statement, and then you will answer with your opinion on the statement, from Strongly Agree to Strongly Disagree, with each answer slightly affecting your scores. At the end of the quiz, your answers will be compared to the maximum possible for each value, thus giving you a percentage. Answer honestly!

There are two versions of the quiz: a full version with 4 questions per axis, for a total of 56 questions, and a short version with 4 questions per axis, for a total of 28 questions. Feel free to take whichever.

What are the 7 axes?

There are nine independent axes, where each has two opposing values assigned to them. They are:

Offence vs. Defence
Offence tends to favour hegemony at all costs, Defence tends to prefer appropriate amount of power.

Independence vs. Interdependence
Independence tends to be content with military power, Interdependence tends to prefer considering the costs of using force.

Irrationalism vs. Rationalism
Irrationalism tends to favour momentary goals, Rationalism tends to prefer maximum utility.

Securitization vs. Politization
Securitization tends to favour moving questions out of the political arena, Politization tends to prefer perception of any question as a matter of politics.

Statism vs. Individualism
Statism tends to favour states as main political figures, Individualism tends to prefer people as a starting point

Actors vs. Structures
Actors tends to favour state as the only carriers of power, Structures tend to favour discources that make us act the way we act.

Nationalism vs. Internationalism
Nationalism tends to prefer independence in decision making, those with highter internationalism scores tend to prefer commonality in decision making up to world government
